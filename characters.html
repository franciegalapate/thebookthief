Characters

The voices and lives that shape The Book Thief.

Liesel Meminger

The story’s heart and soul. Liesel discovers the power of words by stealing, reading, and sharing books. Her resilience and curiosity remind us that even amid fear, learning and storytelling can keep us alive.

Hans Hubermann

Liesel’s foster father. Gentle, kind, and quietly brave, Hans teaches Liesel to read and comforts her with his accordion. His quiet defiance makes him one of the story’s most noble figures.

Rosa Hubermann

Liesel’s foster mother. Tough, blunt, and sharp-tongued—but beneath the bark lies fierce love and loyalty. She shows that care can come in forms we don’t always expect.

Rudy Steiner

Liesel’s best friend. Playful, mischievous, and courageous, Rudy’s devotion and quiet acts of rebellion make him unforgettable.

Max Vandenburg

Hidden by the Hubermanns, Max deepens the story’s exploration of humanity and survival. His friendship with Liesel shows how imagination and words sustain hope.

Death

The narrator. Through Death’s detached yet compassionate voice, we see humanity in contradiction—cruelty and beauty, fragility and endurance.
